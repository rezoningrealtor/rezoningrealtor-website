Developers & Multiplex

Development-Focused Real Estate, Done Right

Development is not just buying and selling—it’s knowing what is truly possible.

Finding the right lot is only the beginning. The real value comes from understanding what you can actually build, what it will cost, and whether the numbers make sense in today’s market.

That’s where experience matters!

With hands-on involvement in lot subdivisions, duplexes, and multiplex projects, I approach every property with a builder’s mindset—not just a sales perspective.

Right now, the opportunity across Greater Vancouver is changing fast. With new provincial policy like Bill 44, many properties that were once limited to single-family homes may now allow for duplexes, triplexes, fourplexes, or more.

For existing homeowners, this can often mean that your property could be worth more than you think and so how you approach selling it matters more than ever.

If you’re a homeowner, I help you uncover your property’s true development potential before you make a decision.
If you’re a first-time builder, I help you evaluate opportunities with clarity and confidence.
If you’re an experienced developer, I help you source and secure sites that make sense.

Move-Up Buyers

Moving Up — From Condo to Townhouse, Townhouse to Detached, or Into Your Forever Home

Moving up sounds straightforward until you're living it. The real complexity is in the sequencing: do you sell first or buy first? How do you avoid carrying two mortgages? What does your next property actually need to look like for the next ten years of your life — not just right now?

These are the conversations I work through with move-up buyers before we start to look at listings. Having done this with dozens of tri-cities families, I know where the traps are, and I know how to navigate around them. The difference between a smooth move-up and a stressful one is almost always the strategy you build before anything goes on the market.

The sell-first vs. buy-first decision alone can cost or save you tens of thousands of dollars. Getting it right requires understanding your equity position, your bridging options, your target neighbourhood's inventory levels, and your risk tolerance. That's the work we do together — before anything is listed.

Sell-first vs. buy-first analysis — done correctly before any listing activity
Bridge financing strategy to avoid carrying two properties
Target neighbourhood assessment — where your budget goes furthest for your next 10 years
Timing strategy — when to list for maximum price and minimum stress
Negotiation on both sides — I've managed both transactions simultaneously for dozens of clients
Talk About Your Move-Up →

The Move-Up Sequence

  • Assess current equity and purchasing power
  • Define the target property — neighbourhood, lot size, living area, must-haves
  • Analyse the target market: inventory, days on market, price trends
  • Decide: sell first or buy first (help with making a well informed decision)
  • Prepare your current property for market
  • Execute both transactions with coordination and timing precision

"The right move-up strategy can save you tens of thousands. The wrong sequence may cost you just as much."

What we look at together:

  • Conservative renovation budgets based on real market costs
  • Rental projections grounded in current comparables, not peak rents
  • Carrying costs — mortgage, strata, taxes, insurance, vacancy
  • Exit strategy defined before entry — flip, rent, or hold
  • Micro-location analysis to identify where value actually holds
  • Comparable sales analysis — where current pricing actually is, not where it was
  • Timeline and delay risks factored into overall returns
  • Clear, numbers-driven go / no-go recommendation
