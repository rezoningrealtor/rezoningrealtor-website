Buying or Selling in Langley

One of the Fraser Valley's fastest-growing communities — with a booming residential market, strong commercial demand, and a new SkyTrain extension reshaping property values.

About Langley

Langley encompasses two distinct municipalities — the City of Langley and the Township of Langley — each with its own character and market dynamics. Together, they form one of the Fraser Valley's most diverse and fastest-growing real estate markets, attracting everyone from young families priced out of Metro Vancouver to investors eyeing the commercial corridor along Highway 1 and 200th Street.

The arrival of the Surrey-Langley SkyTrain extension has been a game-changer. Properties within walking distance of planned stations in Willoughby and the City of Langley have seen a measurable uplift, while the broader Township continues to attract builders and developers responding to strong move-up buyer demand. Langley's light industrial parks also remain among the most sought-after in the Fraser Valley, with low vacancy and rising lease rates.

Langley Market Snapshot

Langley offers some of the best value in Greater Vancouver and the Fraser Valley, with detached homes significantly more affordable than Metro Vancouver while delivering generous lot sizes and newer construction. Here's what the market looks like in 2026:

Langley Neighbourhoods

Langley's sub-markets range from high-density urban infill to large rural acreages. Here's a breakdown of the key areas:

  • WilloughbyLangley's growth engine. Master-planned townhome and condo communities, strong rental demand, and significant SkyTrain station proximity uplift. Best for investors and first-time buyers.
  • Walnut GroveEstablished family neighbourhood with strong schools and parks. Detached homes on generous lots. Stable, consistent market with good resale fundamentals.
  • City of LangleyCompact urban core with walkable downtown, heritage character, and active commercial strip along Fraser Highway. Increasing condo development targeting young professionals.
  • Brookswood / MurrayvilleSpacious lots, quiet streets, semi-rural feel. Popular with buyers seeking acreage or large detached homes. Slower pace of development but strong lifestyle appeal.
  • AldergroveMost affordable entry point in Langley. Older housing stock with development potential. Growing interest from investors as the SkyTrain corridor expands eastward.

Pros and Considerations

Why People Choose Langley

  • More affordable detached homes than Metro Vancouver
  • SkyTrain extension driving value appreciation
  • Strong commercial and light industrial market
  • Excellent schools and family-friendly communities
  • Space, greenery, and equestrian properties available
  • Willoughby among fastest-growing communities in BC

Things to Factor In

  • Car-dependent outside of Willoughby and City core
  • Highway 1 congestion during peak hours
  • SkyTrain still in construction — proximity benefits future-focused
  • Rural areas have limited transit and amenities
