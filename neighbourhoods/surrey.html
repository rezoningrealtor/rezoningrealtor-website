Buying or Selling in Surrey

BC's second-largest city offers some of the most diverse real estate opportunities in Metro Vancouver — from transit-oriented City Centre condos to spacious South Surrey estates.

About Surrey

Surrey is BC's second-largest city by population — and one of its most complex real estate markets. The city spans dramatically different sub-markets, from the high-rise density of Surrey City Centre and Whalley along the King George SkyTrain corridor, to the established family neighbourhoods of Fleetwood and Newton, to the upscale acreage and ocean-view properties of South Surrey and White Rock.

For investors and developers, Surrey's aggressive Official Community Plan is creating significant opportunities. City Centre's TOD (transit-oriented development) zones, combined with the newly extended Surrey-Langley SkyTrain line, have placed a premium on properties within walking distance of stations. Meanwhile, Surrey's commercial real estate market — particularly light industrial and retail — continues to attract strong demand from the Fraser Valley expansion.

Surrey Neighbourhoods

Surrey is a large, diverse city — knowing which pocket you're in changes everything. Here's a breakdown of the main sub-markets:

  • Surrey City Centre / WhalleyRapid densification along the King George SkyTrain corridor. High-rise condos, pre-sales, and strong rental demand. Best for investors and first-time buyers entering the market.
  • FleetwoodEstablished family neighbourhood with newer townhome and condo projects. Close to Fleetwood Park Secondary. Balanced market with consistent demand from move-up buyers.
  • NewtonLarge lots, older housing stock, and strong development potential under SSMUH legislation. Commercial corridor along 72nd Ave. Popular with investors for rental conversions.
  • South Surrey / White RockUpscale detached homes, ocean views, and luxury estates. Higher price points but strong resale. Preferred by retirees, executives, and buyers relocating from Vancouver.
  • CloverdaleHistoric charm with newer master-planned communities. Family-friendly, good schools, and more affordable detached options. Growing commercial strip along 176th St.

Pros and Considerations

Why People Choose Surrey

  • SkyTrain access (King George, Gateway, Surrey Central)
  • More affordable than Vancouver or Burnaby
  • Strong development upside in City Centre TOD zones
  • Diverse, multicultural community
  • Growing commercial and light industrial demand
  • New Surrey-Langley SkyTrain extension adds value

Things to Factor In

  • Wide variation in quality across neighbourhoods
  • Heavy traffic on Highway 99 and King George Blvd
  • Some areas have slower appreciation than Tri-Cities
  • Older housing stock in Newton may need updates
